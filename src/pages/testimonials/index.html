Veronica Carden

I am eagerly writing this letter of recommendation on behalf of Alex Meddin, a dear friend and colleague of mine. I have known Alex for the last three years and have had the pleasure of working with him and becoming a dear friend. Being the founder and director of a non-profit organization I have had the privilege of working with hundreds of volunteers and I am often asked to provide words of recommendation. I generally oblige with no hesitation but as I prepare to write words about Alex I hesitate because I'm not sure that I have the proper vocabulary to articulate how special Alex is. He is brilliant, curious, compassionate and brave. He enters the world of those of us lucky enough to be around him and strategically leaves us better and brighter.

I grew especially close with Alex in the summer of 2020 in our home city of Philadelphia- our Nation was reeling from the devastating effects of COVID, police brutality, poverty and inequality (just to name a few). During the summer of 2020 I was afraid- afraid of what my city was becoming, what my country became- afraid and exhausted from daily protests, teargas and an ever-growing National Guard presence on the corners of blocks that I used to call home. I felt myself shrinking and felt increasingly helpless and then I met Alex.

Alex was a volunteer for my community based mutual aid project and 501c3 called Club Sandwich Philly. Club Sandwich is a project of food and friendship- we work to provide life saving resources to vulnerable individuals through the allocation of material resources while simultaneously advocating for policy change to guarantee unlimited access to these resources for all members of the community. Alex entered the space of Club Sandwich and I could immediately feel his determination to provide comfort, hope and stability during a time of endless confusion and suffering. Alex quickly became our most responsible and dedicated volunteer. He asked questions , he listened, he provided advice, compassion and resources.

He quite literally changed the lives of thousands of Philadelphians- including me. I was overwhelmed and exhausted from my tireless dedication to Club Sandwich and I never felt comfortable taking a break until Alex filled a leadership role within the organization. I have never met anyone as responsible and dedicated and I trusted him implicitly with such important work. Through Alex's leadership and commitment Club Sandwich provided material resources to more than 40,000 individuals in a period of 10 months.

He is incredibly smart, in life and in books. He is a great teacher and friend and I am beyond excited for whichever organizations have the privilege to work with him. He brings a commitment that is steadfast and unwavering. He is reliable, responsible, and kind. He is able to be persuasive and still keep you inspired.

I encourage you to see what he is capable of. He has only made those of us around him better and brighter and I assure you that he will do the same for you.
